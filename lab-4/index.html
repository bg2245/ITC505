Addendum: Stylistic Choices Explanation

In styling the essay, I made several deliberate choices to enhance its visual appeal and readability while maintaining web design best practices. Here's a breakdown of those choices:

  • Font Choices: The font-family is set to 'Segoe UI', a modern and widely available sans-serif font. This choice prioritizes readability and ensures consistency across different devices and platforms.
  • Heading Sizes and Main Text Size: The title of the essay is marked with an <h1> tag to signify its importance as the primary heading. This follows semantic HTML principles and establishes clear hierarchy within the document. Section headings (<h2>) are styled with a font size of 28px to distinguish them from the main text and provide visual hierarchy. The use of a dotted border-bottom adds a decorative element while maintaining readability. The main text size is kept at a standard 16px, h2 balancing readability with efficient use of space.
  • Color Choices: The background color of #f4f4f4 provides a neutral backdrop, enhancing readability without distracting the reader. The header and footer backgrounds feature a gradient effect created with linear gradients. This gradient transition adds visual interest and depth to the header and footer sections. Text color is set to #333, offering good contrast against the background for optimal legibility. Links are styled in black (#000) with no underlines to distinguish them from regular text, ensuring consistency and ease of identification.
  • Margin and Padding Choices: A margin of 20px is applied to the entire document, creating space between the content and the edges of the viewport for a balanced layout. Each section is padded with 20px, providing visual separation and improving overall organization. Headings have a margin-bottom of 20px to establish clear visual hierarchy and flow between sections.
